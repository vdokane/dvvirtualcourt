Question 3

Is it possible that the judge might order temporary child support in a temporary injunction?
You answered Yes. Actually, while the judge can award the petitioner temporary exclusive use and occupancy of the dwelling that the parties share (Section 741.30(5)(a)(2), Florida Statutes) as well as temporary custody of a minor child or children of the parties (Section 741.30(5)(a)(3), Florida Statutes) ex parte, child support cannot be awarded until the final hearing. Find out whether paternity has ever been established and if the petitioner is already receiving child support in another case. Also ask whether the mother was legally married to a man who is not the other party in the current case when the child(ren) was conceived or born. If paternity has never been established, inform the petitioner that initiating a paternity case is one way to have child support established on a permanent basis.
