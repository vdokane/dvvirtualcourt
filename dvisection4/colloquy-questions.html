Colloquy Questions

Question 1

What additional advice should you give Respondents who have a criminal case pending arising from the same facts?
This is correct. All of the statements are true. Respondents with a pending criminal case arising from the same facts have the right to remain silent in the DV proceeding. If a Respondent chooses to testify in the DV proceeding, he or she should be advised that anything they say may be used against them in the criminal case. Respondents should also be advised that even if the DV injunction is dismissed, any conditions imposed on them through the pending criminal case, such as a no-contact order, will remain in effect. Violation of that order will subject them to criminal penalties and possible imprisonment.

Question 2

How may Respondent communicate with Petitioner if an injunction is entered with a no-contact provision?
This is incorrect. No-contact means exactly that-no contact at all between the parties. Respondent cannot contact Petitioner directly or indirectly, through a third party, by phone, text, or email, or through any form of social media. If Respondent arrives at a public place or social gathering and Petitioner is present, Respondent must immediately leave until Petitioner is no longer present. If Respondent contacts Petitioner, he or she violates the injunction.

Question 3

Which of the following statements is NOT true?
This is incorrect. The untrue statement is that determination of time-sharing between parties is permanent. Pursuant to s. 741.30(6), time-sharing ordered between the parties in injunction hearings is temporary in nature; the time-sharing schedule remains in effect until the injunction expires or an order is entered in a pending or subsequent civil case or proceeding involving parental rights and responsibilities for the minor children.
