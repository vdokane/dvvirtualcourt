Question 4

Does the judge have any options other than granting or denying the petition?
You answered Yes. This is correct. The court has three options when considering a petition:
  • Issue the temporary injunction which includes setting the matter for a final hearing;
  • Deny the temporary injunction and provide written explanation for doing so;
  • Enter an Order Setting Hearing Only.
While the Judge may not have found that the petition stated sufficient grounds for the issuance of a temporary injunction, the Judge may still want to speak to the parties in person in order to better understand the allegations in the petition.
