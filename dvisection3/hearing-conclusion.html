Question 1

Must an injunction have an expiration date?
Actually, an injunction may be entered for either a definite or indefinite period. If the injunction is for an indefinite period, the parties should be advised that that either is able to move to vacate the injunction at any time. If the injunction is entered for a definite period of time, the parties should be advised of the expiration date and Petitioner should be advised that if he or she is still in fear of Respondent, a motion to extend the injunction must be filed before it expires.

Question 2

If you dismiss a petition for injunction, should you advise Petitioner of their right to refile?
Actually, Petitioner should be advised that the dismissal is based on a finding that he or she is not a victim of domestic violence or there is not reasonable cause to believe domestic violence is imminent. That decision is based on the evidence presented; however, Petitioner has the right to refile his or her petition if there is future violence or they are in imminent danger of violence.

Question 3

Must Respondent testify in a DV proceeding if there is a pending criminal case?
Actually, respondent may testify in the DV proceeding if he or she wishes, but the testimony may be used in the criminal case; therefore, Respondent should be advised of his or her 5th amendment rights prior to testifying in the DV proceeding.
