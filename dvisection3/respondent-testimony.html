Respondent Testimony

The Respondent, Joe Martin, comes to the stand

Question 1

Must a respondent testify at the final hearing?
Actually, a respondent is not required to testify at the final hearing; the statute requires that he or she be personally served with the petition, financial affidavit, UCCJEA, if any, notice of hearing, and temporary injunction, if any, prior to the hearing. Section 741.30(4).

Question 2

May a respondent invoke their Fifth Amendment privileges during the final hearing?
This is correct. Judges should understand the boundaries of Fifth Amendment issues and should exercise their discretion when moving forward with an injunction proceeding when there is a pending criminal matter against the respondent. Judges should consider the impact on the petitioner and witnesses. Speegle v. Rhoden, 236 So.3d 498, (Fla. 1st DCA 2018). Please refer to the Colloquy video within Section Five, which has suggested remarks for respondents regarding Fifth Amendment concerns.
